## FAQ

### 1. Can I trust this enough to change a real renewal decision?
Yes, as a first-pass audit. The savings logic is deterministic and grounded in plan pricing, seat counts, and tool fit, so it is designed to support a real review before renewal.

### 2. What if my team says they need every tool?
The audit is meant to start a defensible conversation, not force a blind cut. It flags where a downgrade, seat reduction, overlap review, or credit check is worth discussing.

### 3. Will this recommend cutting business-critical tools?
Not automatically. The engine is intentionally conservative and surfaces where spend looks mismatched or duplicative, so a founder or operator can review the risk before making a change.

### 4. How should I use this if we have enterprise pricing or heavy API spend?
Use your real monthly bill as the baseline. The audit still helps identify overlap, plan mismatch, and credit opportunities even when public list-price comparison is less exact.

### 5. When should I involve Credex instead of handling it internally?
If the audit shows meaningful savings and the next step involves vendor negotiation, startup credits, or stack consolidation across teams, Credex is the execution layer after the self-serve audit.
