# Economics

If Credex deployed this tool tomorrow, the key economic question is not whether audits are cheap to generate. They are. The real question is whether completed audits turn into consultations and then into monetizable savings capture. A rough but useful way to model lead value is to start from the downstream credit or procurement outcome. Suppose a booked consultation has a 25% chance of leading to a Credex-supported savings capture event, and suppose that event is worth $2,000 in gross contribution to Credex on average through credit margin, advisory margin, or vendor-side economics. That makes a consultation worth about $500 in expected value. If 20% of captured leads book a consultation, then a captured lead is worth about $100 in expected value.

Channel CAC should be treated differently by source. Community posts and founder threads are mostly time-cost channels, so if a founder spends 6 hours preparing posts and outreach and gets 10 captured leads, even at an internal cost of $40/hour the CAC is about $24 per lead. Warm network outreach could be even cheaper, maybe $10 to $15 per lead equivalent, because response rates are higher. Cold outbound to founders is costlier in time; if 100 messages produce 4 leads and the work takes 5 hours, CAC is closer to $50 per lead. These are still healthy if the expected lead value is near $100.

To see when the tool becomes profitable, use the funnel from audit completed to booked consultation to paid Credex outcome. If 100 completed audits produce 20 captured leads, 4 booked consultations, and 1 paid credit/procurement outcome worth $2,000 gross contribution, that is strong enough to support a low-cost distribution engine. The breakeven bar is not extreme: even a 10% lead capture rate, 15% consultation-booking rate from captured leads, and 20% close rate from consultations can work if each closed outcome is worth at least a few thousand dollars.

For this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, the math likely needs a mix of strong conversion and repeatable distribution. If Credex nets $10,000 ARR-equivalent value per closed customer, it needs about 100 such customers. Spread across 18 months, that is roughly 6 new customers per month on average. If consultation-to-close is 20%, that means 30 consultations per month. If lead-to-consultation is 15%, that means 200 qualified leads per month. If audit completion to qualified lead is 12%, the product needs roughly 1,700 completed audits per month. That is ambitious, but not absurd, especially if the public-share loop works and Credex can pair the tool with community distribution and partner channels.
